Kriya Yoga

Kriya Yoga is an ancient science of meditation and pranayama that accelerates spiritual evolution. Passed through an unbroken lineage of masters, it offers a systematic path to still the mind, awaken inner energy, and realize our unity with the Divine.

The path honors the great beings who have preserved and transmitted these techniques in their purity—from the primordial source of creation through the rishis, Mahavatar Babaji, and the teachers who serve humanity today. Below we remember the pillars of our lineage with gratitude and reverence.

Our Lineage

Para Parameshti Guru Maha Sadashiva

Maha Sadashiva represents the supreme consciousness—the eternal source from which all creation flows. In Kriya Yoga, this highest principle is honored as Para Parameshti Guru, the ultimate teacher beyond form, whose presence is realized in deep meditation as peace, wisdom, and unconditional love.

Parameshti Guru Saptarishi Agastya

Among the great Saptarishis, Agastya is revered as a pillar of wisdom who helped shape spiritual and cultural life across ages. As Parameshti Guru, he stands in the lineage as an enlightened master through whom profound knowledge—including aspects of yoga and healing sciences—has been safeguarded and shared for the upliftment of seekers everywhere.

Parama Guru Mahavatar Babaji

Mahavatar Babaji is venerated as an immortal yogi who revives and protects Kriya Yoga for each generation. His life embodies complete mastery over prana and consciousness. Through his grace, the ancient science of Kriya has reached sincere devotees worldwide, offering a direct, practical way to experience inner stillness and divine communion.

Sadguru Dr. Siju

With a background in medicine and a heart turned toward the Divine, Sadguru Dr. Siju bridges compassionate service and deep spiritual practice. His guidance helps students apply Kriya Yoga in daily life—balancing health, duty, and inner awakening—while remaining rooted in the humility and discipline of the lineage.
